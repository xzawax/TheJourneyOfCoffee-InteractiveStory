Where It All Begins

Coffee grows only within the Bean Belt — a narrow equatorial band stretching between the Tropics of Cancer and Capricorn. Rich volcanic soil, high altitude, and consistent rainfall create the perfect conditions for Coffea arabica and Coffea robusta to thrive.

Picked by Hand

Each coffee cherry is handpicked at peak ripeness — a bright, deep red. Skilled pickers can strip an entire branch or selectively choose only the ripest cherries. This labour-intensive process defines the cup quality before anything else happens.

03

The Defining Moment

Heat transforms a pale green bean into the aromatic brown one we know. The Maillard reaction produces over 800 distinct aroma compounds. Roast level — light, medium, or dark — shapes everything: acidity, sweetness, bitterness, and body.

Dial In Your Ratio

The coffee-to-water ratio is the single biggest variable under your control. The Specialty Coffee Association recommends 1:16–1:18 for filter brewing — but taste is subjective. Use the calculator to find your sweet spot.

A stronger ratio amplifies body and bitterness. Going lighter opens up delicate floral and fruity notes that bold brews can mask.
